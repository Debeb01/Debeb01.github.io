Research

Kennesaw State University AERO Lab
CAPSTONE

Developing a Life Jacket Delivery System for UAVs

Designed a servo-actuated payload release mechanism to carry and accurately deploy a life jacket from a multirotor UAV, aimed at cutting response time in drowning emergencies where surveillance and traditional flotation delivery can't reach. Iterated the design in SOLIDWORKS, settled on a linkage-based latch driven by an MG996R torque servo, and validated it with a successful flight test. FEA on the final assembly found the linkage bar reached its allowable stress at a 6.5 kg load — below the 25 kg carrying-capacity target — identifying where the next design iteration needs to strengthen the mechanism.

AIAA

"Real-Time Vehicle Detection and Enumeration Using Unmanned Aerial Systems"

Built a real-time vehicle detection and counting framework combining the YOLOv12 object detection model with aerial imagery from a UAS platform, fine-tuned on the VisDrone dataset to handle varying altitudes, lighting, and dense urban scenes. Achieved detection accuracy and frame rates suitable for real-time use, aimed at automated traffic analytics and aerial surveillance applications.

AIAA Aviation Forum · San Diego, CA · June 8–12, 2026 Co-authors: O. Ahmed, A. Khalid Read paper (DOI) →
